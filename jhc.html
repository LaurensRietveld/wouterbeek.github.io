A Theory of Justice for Health Care

Three Questions:

  • Is health care (morally) special?
  • When are health inequalities unjust?
  • How can we meet competing health care needs fairly under reasonable resource constraint?

No consensus on distribution

Fair process?

Opportunity for Equality:

Health care is morally special

  • Central role is to maintain normal functioning
  • Protection of equality of opportunity

Determinants of Health

Factors

  • Wealth
  • Race and Class

Social policies are responsible for social and economic inequalities that produce health effects -> justice as fairness of distribution of medical goods.

In absence of consensus on distribute principles, we need a fair process to establish legitimacy for critical resource allocation decisions. Daniel's proposition: "accountability for reasonability"

Question 1: what is the special moral importance of health care?

  • Normal Functioning -> normal range of opportunity as citizens (relative)
  • Plans of life (in a perfect world)

Relationship between protection of opportunity and health care implies regulations appropriate to this principle.

Social contractors must be assured fair equality of opportunity in access to jobs and offices.

Opportunity over happiness

  • More objective measure
  • Implies index primary social goods: rights, liberties, powers and opportunities, income and wealth, social bases of self-respect.

Protection of normal functioning within the scope of primary good of opportunity.

Implies restoring, not enhancing.

Other primary needs

  • Paternalistic to place emphasis on health care
  • Rebuttal: 'Our' social obligations (as social contractors) is to provide institutions that protect opportunity, not to maximize aggregate welfare.

Universal access to appropriate care, preventive and traditional.

Limited resources --> hierarchy in needs.

Social factors

  • Requires focus on more than traditional health care: prevention "Ambulance waiting at te bottom of the cliff"

Daniel's conjecture: equal liberties + robustly equal opportunity + fair distribution of resources + support for our self-esteem --> eliminate to important degree injustices in health outcomes.

Basis for conjecture

  • Life expectancy in richer countries
  • Life expectancy between equally poor countries
  • Relative deprivation (both income and general)

Individual level: socioeconomic gradient: with each step higher up on the economic ladder we see improved health outcomes. The slope, however, is fixed by the level of income: the more unequal in economic terms, the more unequal in health terms.

But how to allocate resources?

  • Earlier principles make it easier to address inequality in outcomes, but doesn't solve disagreement on how to distribute resources.
